Research Interest

I'm primarily interested in discovering novel magnetic materials (focusing on Heusler alloys) for spintronic application, especially for memory and storage devices. The material of interest is half-metal, a material with a possibility of exhibiting both metallic and insulating (or semiconducting) behavior in different spin channels. I'm an experimentalist who synthesizes samples and characterizes various properties. However, I also enjoy studying the ground state properties by performing first principle calcuations. Solving problems by employing statistical methods is what I've always relished. I also like to write code (mainly python) to solve moderate to complex issues. Currently, I am working on solving the issue in material science via machine learning.

Throughout my career as a graduate student, I've been involved in several other research projects as well, such as investigating consequences of metal insulator transition in VO2, and using antiferromagnetic insulator (i.e., NiO) to measure the spin diffusion length.

Below, I describe a few selected projects that I've been involved on; the project goal, research methodology, and major accomplishments.

Project: First-Principles Based Design of Spintronics Materials and Devices (NSF DMREF # 1235396)

Goal

Using first principle calculations (DFT) discover new candidate half-metals and synthesize the candidate half-metals experimentally to test its phase stability, structural, electrical, magnetic and mechanical characteristics. If the material is promising, employ more advanced techniques (such as fabrication of thin film) to probe dynamic magnetic and spin polarization properties.

Project: Investigation of spin-pumping and magneto-elastic coupling in ferromagnetic/VO2 heterostructures (NSF DMR # 1508680)

Goal

Vandium dioxide (VO2) undergoes ultrafast switching from a low-temperature electrical insulator into a high-temperature electrical conductor accompanied by a dramatic change in the electrical conductivity. Due this switching behavior, it is an ideal material to make transistors, which is the building block of almost all of the electrical devices. The transition is also responsible for the dramatic change in susceptibility. As a consequence of susceptibility change, enhancement of spin-pumping and a change in magnetic properties are expected upon crossing the transition temperature of VO2.

Research Methodology

Experimental

  • Fabrication of VO2 thin films (CVD - low pressure)
  • Deposition of ferromagnetic layer (PVD - magnetron sputtering)
  • Thickness and structural analysis (XRD, XRR)
  • Surface roughness analysis (AFM)
  • Resistivity measurements (four probes)
  • Spin-pumping measurement (FMR)

Notable Achievements

  • (Poster-Presenter) Epitaxial Growth and Characterization of VO2 Thin Films on TiO2 Substrates
    MINT Reivew, University of Alabama 2018, Tuscaloosa, AL USA

Project: Spin transport in NiO measured with ferromagnetic resonance

Goal

As spin current has many adavantages over the charge current, such as no Joule's heating, a better understanding of how the spin current transmits and how it dissipates in a medium is crucial to exploit the full extent of spintronic applications. The study of transmission of spin current in an antiferromangetic (AFM) insulator, such as NiO is very interesting since the AMF insulator offers zero stary field, and it is robust against external field. The spin currents can be pumped into the AFM from a ferromagnetic layer so that it propagates through the AFM and depending upon the thickness of AFM layer and its effect can be realized in a non-magnetic layer adjacent to the AFM layer. The aim is to determine the spin diffusion length in NiO, using trilayers of Fe20Ni80/NiO/Pt with ferromagnetic resonance (FMR) measurements. The Pt serves as a spin sink, which means when deposited directly on Fe20Ni80 it strongly increases the effective damping parameter. But with NiO between the Pt and Fe20Ni80, the increase in damping parameter diminishes, and hence the decay length can be extracted from measurements as a function of NiO thickness.

Research Methodology

Experimental

  • Fabrication of trilayers of Fe20Ni80/NiO/Pt (PVD - magnetron sputtering)
  • In-situ surface analysis (RHEED, AES)
  • Structure analysis and thickness measurement (XRD, XRR)
  • Magnetization dynamics (FMR measurement)

Notable Achievements

  • (Poster-Presenter (Arjun Sapkota)) Spin Transport in NiO measured with Ferromangetic Resonance
    AVS 66th International Symposium & Exhibition 2019, Columbus, OH USA
