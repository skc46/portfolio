SHAMBHU KC

Doctoral Candidate | Physics | Material Science | Data Analysis | Artificial Intelligence | Web Design

Shambhu KC is a result-oriented and motivated doctoral student in experimental Condensed Matter Physics at the University of Alabama. He is currently working under the guidance of Dr. Patrick LeClair, and Dr. Arunava Gupta. He recieved B.Sc. in Physics, Statistics, and Mathematics in 2010 from Tribhuvan University, Nepal. Then he recieved M.Sc. in Physics in 2013 from Tribhuvan University, Nepal, where he worked under the supervision of Dr. Binil Aryal to complete his Master's thesis, titled Interaction Between Ambient ISM and AGB Starts at Lattitude -1°. He also obtained his second M.Sc. in experimental Condensded Matter Physics from the University of Alabama in 2019.

Shambhu's main research is focused on the discovery of novel half-metallic Heusler compounds for potential spintronic applications. He excels in sample preparation, characterization, and high throughput data analysis. Besides the research activites, Shambhu enjoys learning and trying new skills, exploring new advancemnts made in the electronic device components. He has received Outstanding Graduate Student Research by a Doctoral Student Award in 2020 from the department of Physics and Astronomy, and in the same year he also received Graduate Council Fellowship from the Graduate School of the University of Alabama.

Shambhu is afiliated with American Physical Society (APS), American Vacuum Society (AVS), and IEEE Magnetics Society. He is also an acting Condensed Matter Experiment Liaison in Physics Astronomy Graduate Student Association (PAGSA) of University of Alabama.
